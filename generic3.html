    Why I Support the Vt-Shaped Student Module

    I think the Vt-shaped student module is very important for students to learn about because many individuals do not know that it exists but possess many of the characteristics that truly make one. I believe that technology should be incorporated into student's learning despite major. Being an english major one might think that I would not use technology but I use a computer everyday for work and am currently writing this in html as I construct my own website. It is important for individuals to be able to effectively use technology as we live in such a digital age however, in some majors they value technology so much that they lose sight of other aspects like the V-shaped individual's characteristics that truly make a well-rounded hokie. The Vt-shaped student module is great because it incorporates aspects of both the V and the T in order to effectively make better rounded students. Like Professor Evia once said anyone can construct a website but without content and the power of words and expression that website is just code. The T-shaped student module is merely code but incorporated with the V there is emphasis on adding content and freedom of expression. It is easy to be intimidated at a University like Virginia Tech when the majority of your classmates in your grade are engineers and there is a heavy emphasis on the math and sciences when you are pursuing a degree in the liberal arts. The amount of times someone has come up to me when I was doing homework for this class and said, "Wait, are you coding right now?" and continued rudely, "Aren't you an english major?" Well, yes I am an english major and I know how to code and I made my own website so I do emulate the full package of the VT-shaped student. Boom.
