What I Learned From This Glorious Project

As I stated earlier if you had told me at the beginning of this school year that I would be making a webpage I would have laughed in your face. However, here I am currently drafting my own website with the help of Professor Evias but it is mostly me!! The topic of the VT shaped student was a foreign one to me before I took this class but I find that it resounds with me as I am an English major more specifically a Literature and Language major at a University that is very geared toward the math and sciences and specifically engineering. Professor Evias brought up an excellent point one day in class when he spoke about a website created with code but it was lacking content and I feel that's where I come in I am the toppings on the pizza. In reality, without the content of a website code is merely code it has no significant value or symbol of worth. The VT Shaped Student is comprised of well-rounded individuals that not only excel in technological aspects but are also passionate people that are willing to embrace diversity and have a strong set of moral values. President Sands embraced the ideal of the VT shaped individual wholeheartedly as the T-shaped student already existed by the V made the model more enriched and specific to Virginia Tech as a whole. I think the most interesting and compelling thing I found about this project was that it was so hands on and forced me to be able to confidently work with HTML. I had no idea how to work HTML before this class. I honestly would freak out on some of my homework assignments and beg my computer science engineer friends to aid me with my work or at least break it down for me. However, once I realized that once you have a template the majority of the things you focus on are content based I was pleasantly surprised. I would be frustrated in the beginning when I would mess up my code or felt lost in the class but now every time I hit refresh and I change a portion of my format in my template it is like a mini victory. I would get very worried that I would accidently delete something and break my template and therefore the internet but it has been going rather swimmingly. The most challenging aspect of HTML was definitely trying to figure out how to put in a link to a website which, I found particularly trying. I am no longer intimidated by using HTML like I originally had been and it makes me feel smarter when I am working with atom. In the beginning it took a great deal of patience in order to complete these tasks in atom but now that I actually know how to use HTML affectively it is interesting. My notion of "digital writing" has drastically changed due to Unit 1 as I have always taken web content for granted now that I am aware how much effort goes into orchestrating a website I will appreciate the small intricacies.
